Kerala is Burning!!

THIRUVANANTHAPURAM:
04/04/2024

Due to the extreme heat in the state, the India Meteorological Department (IMD) has issued a warning for 12 districts in Kerala, anticipating high maximum temperatures over the next five days. There may be a heat wave in some parts of the districts of Kollam, Palakkad, and Thrissur, according to the IMD and the Kerala State Disaster Management Authority.Keralites have been caught unawares by the unprecedented rise in temperatures, heat wave conditions and sunstroke deaths. The Indian Meteorological Department has warned that the heat wave will continue for two more days. With relative humidity reaching 70 percent, the citizens feel virtually suffocated. Earlier, Palakkad district was prone to extreme heat, but now Wayanad district is also experiencing it.

Maximum tempratures are very likely to be around 41°C in palakkad district,around 40°C in Kollam & Thrissur districts,around 38° in Kottayam,Pathanamthitta,Kozhikkode & Kannur districts, around 37°C in Alappuzha,Ernakulam,Malappuram & Kasargod districts, and around 36°C in Thiruvananthapuram district during 28th April to 2nd May 2024, the weather agencies said.

At a time when Kerala is reeling under one of its harshest summers, – following prolonged dry spells and erratic weather patterns – the state is also staring at a major challenge in terms of inadequate weather readings and infrastructure. Adding to the woes, experts point out that the national criteria for declaring heatwaves are not suited for Kerala’s weather patterns. High humidity levels, brought on by its tropical climate, make summers worse in the state, they say.
